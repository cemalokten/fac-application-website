Introduction

Hi, my name is Cemal (Je-mal)(he/him), I live in South London with my partner and our dog Bobby.

My father is from Turkey and my Mother is from London. I grew up on a council estate and left for London to study 3D Design in 2006. After graduating I started working as an assistant at a design studio making furniture.

Since then I have worked as a designer for over 10 years, I‚Äôve designed and made interiors, sculptures for Turner Prize winners and most recently Sunglasses that were worn by Rita Ora (so proud).

I have also had the opportunity to plan and teach twice yearly workshops at Bath Spa University with 1st and 2nd Year 3D students, which I have really loved.

During that time I have often been at odds with what I do, wanting to create inclusive things to help people, but instead creating exclusive things for wealthy people. Teaching was a breath of fresh air.

The global pandemic happened and then lockdown and with that the loss of my part-time job and (very) small sunglasses business. No longer sure of anything, I started to explore the things that I used to love before life got in the way. I researched and talked to alot of people and finally I remembered the thrill of making a piece of code work and seeing it transform a webpage in front of my eyes, something I hadn't done since I was 15.

And here I am, after a very difficult but transformative year, I have come to terms with no longer wanting to be a designer, being a beginner again and embracing everything I can. I have realised that I love to create things, whether it's from a piece of wood or lines of code. I am applying to the Founders and Coders in the hope I can learn, make a difference and together make a world we are all happy to live in.

values and beliefs

I want to live in a world which is inclusive, not exclusive, where being different is celebrated. I want to create things that help other people.

Why Coding?

When I was young my Dad showed me how to create a simple webpage in Dreamweaver. The realisation that I could link one page to another was amazing. From there I taught myself HTML and CSS by looking at the source code of other sites I liked, which is still my favorite way to understand how things work, by breaking them apart and trying to put them back together.

I love solving problems I love creating something from nothing If I can imagine it then I can (hopefully) build it I like to work as a team and coding is all about collaboration

With coding, even when you know how something works, it can still feel like magic.

Why FAC?

Peer led. From experience teaching, working together is always better than alone.

Community. For so long I have wanted to be part of something bigger than myself, part of a community that supports and includes everyone.

Diverse and Inclusive. I've always felt a little bit between,and by that I mean not quite enough, not quite Turkish enough, not quite English enough. I struggle to know how to describe or label myself; a name that almost everyone struggles to say and assumes I am foreign, a voice and appearance that people assume I am English. Founders and Coders feels like a place where it's ok to be different, to be between.
