About

Hi, my name is Cemal (Je-mal) (he/him), I live in South London with my partner and our dog Bobby.

I have two amazing sisters, our Dad is a political exile from Turkey and Mum is from London. We grew up on a council estate and I moved to London to study 3D Design in 2006. After graduating I started working as an assistant making furniture ðŸ”¨.

Since then I have worked as a designer, Iâ€™ve designed and made furniture, interiors, sculptures and some sunglasses that were worn by Rita Ora (so proud). I have also had the opportunity to teach twice yearly design workshops at Bath Spa University, which I really loved.

During that time I have often felt very stuck, wanting to create inclusive things to help people, but instead creating exclusive things for wealthy people. Teaching was a breath of fresh air.

The global pandemic happened and with that the loss of my part-time job. I started to explore the things that I used to love before life got in the way. I remembered the thrill of making a piece of code work and seeing it transform a webpage in front of my eyes, something I hadn't done since I was 15.

And here I am, after a very difficult but transformative year, I have come to terms with no longer wanting to be a designer, being a beginner again and embracing everything I can. I love to create things, whether it's from a planks of wood or lines of code.

    Why FAC?

    Peer led - For me working together is always better than working alone.

    Community - For so long I have wanted to be part of something bigger than myself, part of a community that supports and includes everyone.

    Diverse and Inclusive - I've always felt a little bit between,and by that I mean not quite enough, not quite Turkish enough, not quite English enough. I struggle to know how to describe or label myself; a name that almost everyone struggles to say and assumes I am foreign, a voice and appearance that people assume I am English. Founders and Coders feels like a place where it's ok to be different, to be between.
